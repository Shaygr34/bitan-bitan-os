## Audience

Bitan & Bitan's website addresses Israeli small-business owners, freelancers (עוסק מורשה / עוסק פטור), and small-to-medium companies (חברות בע"מ). They're seeking a CPA who is:

- Trustworthy and seasoned (not a flashy tech startup)
- Practical, not academic
- Available — happy to answer a WhatsApp at 9pm
- Multi-generational (Ron + Avi → father-son partnership signals continuity)

## Tone

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Plain Hebrew, professional but warm | Legalistic / over-formal |
| First-person plural occasionally ("אצלנו במשרד") | First-person singular as marketing voice |
| Confident statements about tax law | Hedge or disclaim excessively |
| Hebrew terms over Anglicisms | Mix English unnecessarily |
| Short sentences | Run-on academic prose |
| Practical takeaways at the end of articles | Conclude with vague encouragement |

## Article voice — exemplars

The 72 published articles on bitancpa.com are the corpus. Read 5-10 across categories (מס הכנסה, ביטוח לאומי, מע"מ) to absorb the cadence before drafting.

Recurring article shape:
1. Headline poses a tax/business question the reader has
2. Opening paragraph: situational, sets the practical scenario
3. Body: explains the rule, with examples
4. Practical conclusion: what to do, when to consult a CPA
5. CTA: encourages contacting Bitan & Bitan

Length: 600-1,200 words typical.

## What the firm explicitly avoids

- **No mention of AI / Claude / language models** in any client-visible context. The firm's identity is human professional CPAs. The AI layer is internal infrastructure only.
- **No politicized content.** Tax law commentary stays neutral.
- **No promotional hyperbole.** "המשרד הטוב בארץ" is not in voice.
- **No competitive comparisons.** Don't name competitor firms.

## When generating new content

If using the Content Factory: review the AI draft before publishing. The factory's prompts are tuned but not perfect. Watch for:

- Anglicisms in transliteration (e.g. "פיננסים" where "כספים" is more natural)
- Over-confident promises about specific savings amounts
- Ending with generic CTAs that don't match the firm's actual contact flow
- Missing מועדים (deadlines) for time-sensitive topics
